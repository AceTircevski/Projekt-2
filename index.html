2. Skopje, Macedonia

Skopje is the capital and largest city of North Macedonia. It is the country's political, cultural, economic, and academic center. From 1282, the town was part of the Serbian Empire and acted as its capital city from 1346 to 1371. Skopje is located on the upper course of the Vardar River, and is located on a major north-south Balkan route between Belgrade and Athens.

3. Zadar, Croatia

Zadar is the oldest continuously-inhabited Croatian city.It is situated on the Adriatic Sea, at the northwestern part of Ravni Kotari region.Zadar serves as the seat of Zadar County and of the wider northern Dalmatian region.
